Valuing the American Dream

A Linear Regression Analysis of the 1970s Boston Housing Market

Executive Summary

This report analyzes the 1970s Boston Housing dataset to identify key drivers of home values using multiple linear regression. The most significant factors are the number of rooms per dwelling (RM), which positively impacts value, and the percentage of the local population with lower socioeconomic status (LSTAT), which has a strong negative impact. Our final model explains approximately 75% of the variation in home prices (Adjusted RÂ² of 0.752). While the dataset's age and ethical issues prevent its use for modern forecasting, the analysis provides timeless strategic insights for real estate investment and urban policy, underscoring how investments in public safety, environmental quality, and education can create tangible economic value.

Critical Limitations

  • The "1978 Problem": Data is over 40 years old and irrelevant for current price prediction.
  • Ethical Flaws: Variables like 'B' were constructed on racist assumptions, encoding bias into the dataset.
  • Statistical Violations: The data violates key linear regression assumptions like linearity and constant variance of errors.
  • Omitted Variables: Fails to account for modern value drivers like interior quality, walkability, or climate risk.
